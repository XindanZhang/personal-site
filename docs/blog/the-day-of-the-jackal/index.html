REVIEW

The day of the Jackal

Published December 2, 2024 review, film, tv

Notes on performance, pacing, and moral tension in the show.

The male lead is so charismatic. His acting is great, and I believed him completely as the Jackal.

He assassinates people for money, disguises himself well, and keeps moving through the story with a strange mix of precision and calm. I expected him to kill his wife after she discovered part of his secret, but he did not. Instead he lied again and kept deceiving her, which made his attachment to family feel even more unsettling.

The shots are stunning and the pace of the story is strong. At least two families are damaged by the work their parents do. Jackal leaves his family in order to survive and keep killing for a living. Bianca leaves her family to keep chasing him. Both lives are distorted by the job, and that contrast lands well.

The one part that stopped me from giving the show a full score was the female lead’s behavior. As an MI6 officer, she intimidates a woman through her daughter, who is only preparing for college. That choice indirectly causes the young girl’s death. I could not look past it, and it made several of her scenes difficult to watch.
